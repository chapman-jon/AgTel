# Trip report — Prairie Ag Expo 2026, Saskatoon

*Author: B. Sorenson (consulting, SK area) — circulated to consulting leadership*
*Event dates: 2026-06-16 to 2026-06-18*

Quick notes from three days working our booth and walking the floor.

## Booth traffic

Steady all three days, heaviest Tuesday afternoon. The spray-drone demo next
door helped us more than it helped them — everyone who watched it wandered
over to us afterward. We collected 140-ish contact cards (handed to the sales
ops intern for entry; flagged the ones I'd call first).

## What growers asked about

1. **Input financing** — far and away the top topic given where grain prices
   sit. Our new financing partnership got genuine interest; two growers asked
   if it stacks with early-pay discounts (it does, within limits — sales ops
   confirmed).
2. **Biologicals** — skepticism remains high ("show me a strip trial on my
   soil"). Good opening for the consulting pitch; I booked four fall visits
   on the spot.
3. **The app question** — a handful of tech-forward growers asked when we're
   getting "something on the phone." Told them it's in the works for
   consultants first. They were fine with that; mostly they want their
   consultant to show up with the data, not to poke at it themselves.

## Competitor notes

- Regional co-op booth pushed hard on their loyalty program restructure.
- The equipment dealer alliance is bundling agronomy-lite services with
  sprayer leases now. Watch this in the western belt.

## Follow-ups

- Fall visit list to area lead (done)
- Financing stacking one-pager would be genuinely useful for field season —
  suggested to sales enablement
- We should bring the taller banner stand next year; ours disappears behind
  the drone cage
