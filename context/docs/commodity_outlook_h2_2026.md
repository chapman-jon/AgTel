# H2 2026 Commodity & Input Outlook (internal digest)

*Market Intelligence — quarterly digest for consulting and sales leadership*
*Prepared June 2026. Not for external distribution. Sources: USDA WASDE, StatsCan,
ABARES, internal purchasing data.*

## Grain markets

- **Corn:** carryout projections loosened through spring; December futures have
  traded a narrow band since April. Basis in the western belt remains firm on
  rail logistics. Watch pollination-window weather — the market has no premium
  priced in.
- **Soybeans:** South American harvest pressure has mostly cleared. Crush margins
  healthy; domestic demand steady. Export pace the swing factor as always.
- **Wheat (spring):** Canadian prairie moisture improved late; quality spreads
  narrower than last year. Australian new-crop (their 2026-27) planting
  intentions up modestly in WA.

## Input costs

- **Nitrogen:** natural gas softness has UAN and urea trending down vs last
  fall's contracts. Purchasing recommends staggered fall fill rather than early
  lock-in; guidance memo to follow in July.
- **Phosphates:** still elevated; no relief expected before 2027 on current
  trade posture.
- **Crop protection:** generic pressure continues on two of our highest-volume
  herbicide actives. Pricing committee reviewing consultant discount bands for
  the 2027 program year.

## What this means for consulting conversations

Growers are cash-flow tighter than the last two seasons. Expect more pushback
on premium program tiers; lead with agronomic ROI, not product features. The
fall financing partnership (announced at the sales kickoff) gives consultants
an additional lever — training materials on the sales enablement site.
