# IT Security Advisory — Targeted phishing campaign (ACTION REQUIRED)

*Information Security Office — advisory 2026-11*
*Issued: 2026-06-22. Audience: all staff. Severity: HIGH.*

## What's happening

Since mid-June we have observed a phishing campaign targeting agricultural
companies, including at least two emails that reached AgTel inboxes before
filters caught up. The emails impersonate **equipment dealers and grain
buyers**, reference plausible field-season business ("updated delivery
schedule," "revised settlement sheet"), and carry either a credential-harvest
link or a macro-enabled spreadsheet.

## What to do

- **Do not open unexpected attachments**, even during field season when
  settlement sheets and delivery schedules are flying around. Verify by phone
  using a number you already have — not one from the email signature.
- Report suspicious emails with the **Report Phish** button (Outlook toolbar).
  Reporting beats deleting; it feeds the filters.
- If you clicked or opened something: contact the ISO hotline immediately.
  No blame — speed matters far more than embarrassment.

## Notes for field staff

- The campaign includes SMS variants ("your load has been rescheduled, click
  to confirm"). Same rules apply on phones.
- Public expo Wi-Fi (it's farm show season): use the VPN, or your hotspot.

## Reminders

- MFA prompts you didn't initiate = someone has your password. Deny and call
  the hotline.
- Password manager rollout continues; enrollment is self-service on the IT
  portal.
